Which degrees command the highest starting salaries for fresh graduates in Singapore? Based on the latest 2025 Graduate Employment Survey (GES) data, here are the top 10 degrees by gross monthly median salary.

## The Top 10

| Rank | Degree | University | School | Median Salary |
|------|--------|-----------|--------|--------------|
| 1 | Medicine | NTU | Lee Kong Chian School of Medicine | $6,500 |
| 2 | Bachelor of Medicine and Bachelor of Surgery | NUS | Yong Loo Lin School of Medicine | $6,500 |
| 3 | Bachelor of Computing (Computer Science) | NUS | School of Computing | $6,400 |
| 4 | Computer Science (Cum Laude and above) | SMU | School of Computing & Information Systems | $6,250 |
| 5 | Bachelor of Business Administration | NUS | NUS Business School | $6,000 |
| 6 | Bachelor of Computing (Information Security) | NUS | School of Computing | $6,000 |
| 7 | Bachelor of Computing (Information Systems) | NUS | School of Computing | $6,000 |
| 8 | Computer Science | SMU | School of Computing & Information Systems | $6,000 |
| 9 | Information Systems (Cum Laude and above) | SMU | School of Computing & Information Systems | $5,785 |
| 10 | Bachelor of Science (Business Analytics) | NUS | School of Computing | $5,700 |

## Key Takeaways

**Medicine and computing dominate.** The top two spots go to medical degrees from NTU and NUS, both at $6,500 median gross monthly salary. But the rest of the top 10 is entirely computing and tech-related degrees.

**NUS School of Computing leads in tech.** Four of the top 10 are from NUS School of Computing, with Computer Science graduates earning a median of $6,400 per month.

**SMU Computer Science competes closely.** SMU's Computer Science graduates (Cum Laude and above) earn $6,250, and the overall cohort earns $6,000 -- very close to NUS.

**Business Analytics crosses the $5,700 mark.** NUS's Business Analytics degree, which blends computing with business skills, rounds out the top 10.

## About the Data

The Graduate Employment Survey is conducted jointly by NTU, NUS, SIT, SMU, SUSS and SUTD approximately six months after graduation. Gross monthly salary comprises basic salary, overtime payments, commissions, fixed allowances and other regular cash payments, before deductions of CPF contributions and personal income tax.

Salary figures represent the median (50th percentile) among full-time permanently employed graduates who responded to the survey.
